DBMS 2 Course Final Project: Community Game Hub

The "Community Game Hub" served as the capstone project for my Database Management Systems course during the third term of BIT. This platform was meticulously designed to cater to video game enthusiasts, facilitating a robust environment where users could explore, review, and track their gaming experiences. The core functionality of the project involved managing extensive records including video games, user profiles, game reviews, user game collections, and user achievements. This allowed users to not only find and review games but also manage a personalized collection of games they owned or planned to purchase, while unlocking achievements based on their platform activities. I chose this project for my ePortfolio to highlight the comprehensive skills and knowledge I developed in database management, utilizing a variety of technologies including SSMS for SQL, and MongoDB for NoSQL databases, covering advanced topics like data manipulation, indexing, and complex query management.
